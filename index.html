Comprehensive Scholarship Benefits

The MSRS program is designed to provide a full financial package to ensure scholars can focus on their studies.

Tuition & School Fees

Full coverage of tuition and other school fees, paid directly to the university.

Living & Lodging

Includes a Living Subsidy (PHP 30,000) and Lodging Allowance (PHP 30,000) per semester.

Academic Allowances

Covers books (PHP 13,000), uniforms (PHP 3,500), and miscellaneous/connectivity (PHP 6,000) per semester.

Transportation

Provides a Transportation Allowance of PHP 6,000 per semester (PHP 1,000/month).

Career Pathway

One-time grants for Internship (PHP 132,000/year) and Board Review/Licensure (PHP 16,000).

Health & Accident Insurance

Enrollment in PhilHealth and a GSIS Accident Insurance plan, with premiums paid by CHED.

Scholar Experience & Survey Findings

Overwhelming Reliance on Stipend

Severe Consequences of Delays

Primary Coping Mechanisms

  • Borrowing Money: The most common strategy, with 82% borrowing from family/friends and 24% taking formal loans.
  • Strict Budgeting: Forced to "significantly cut back on expenses" and delay buying required uniforms, books, and equipment.
  • Reducing Food Intake: Many scholars (55%) explicitly skip meals to save money, with one stating, "I do not eat breakfast to save my allowance."
  • Seeking Additional Income: Several scholars have had to find part-time jobs while enrolled in a demanding medical program.

Pro-Tip: Use a Requirement Tracker

A successful practice has been the use of a shared spreadsheet (like Google Sheets) to track each scholar's submission status. This creates transparency and allows student representatives to quickly identify who is missing a document, which is crucial since one person can delay the entire batch. We highly recommend all batches adopt this system to improve coordination and accountability.

Proposed Solutions

Based on survey data and direct scholar feedback, here are paths forward.

Key Scholar Recommendations

1. Improve Process Efficiency

  • Appoint additional staff dedicated solely to the MSRS program.
  • Process applications individually or by batch, not for the entire group at once.
  • Establish and adhere to a strict schedule for every step of the process.
  • Begin processing 3 months before classes to ensure timely receipt.

2. Increase Transparency

  • Create an accessible status tracker or flowchart for scholars.
  • Provide constant, clear, and specific updates (no vague statements).
  • Designate a point person per batch for easier communication.

3. Bypass Bureaucracy

  • Enable direct deposit from CHED to scholars' bank accounts.
  • Evaluate and change the disbursing bank if it would expedite the process.

4. Enhance Institutional Support

  • Provide clear instructions, requirements, and deadlines well in advance.
  • The university must offer interest-free loans as an official stop-gap measure.

Specific Proposal: The MSRS Advance Program

As part of enhancing institutional support, this program would provide a low-friction bridge fund. It is an advance, not a loan, built on the scholar's existing contract. Here is the simplified workflow:

1
Onboarding & Activation

Scholar signs MSRS contract and a one-time Advance Agreement to enroll.

2
Monthly Advance

The university releases a fixed, predictable advance to the scholar each month.

3
Stipend Arrival & Repayment

When the CHED stipend arrives, the scholar repays the advanced amount to replenish the fund.

Program Advantages vs. Existing Loan System

This table clarifies why the MSRS Advance is the superior, purpose-built solution.

Feature MSRS Advance Program Existing UPMFI Student Loan
Application Process One-time, streamlined agreement at the start of the scholarship. Requires a full, separate loan application process.
Required Documents None. Uses existing scholar data on file. Involves redundant requirements like new forms, and photocopies of student and parent IDs.
Parental Involvement Not required, as it is an advance on the scholar's own stipend. Requires a Parent's Consent and Guarantee Letter.
Core Principle An integrated advance to bridge a systemic delay. A loan to solve what is framed as a personal financial issue.
Administration Managed by the MSRS office, which has full context of the scholars' situation. Managed by a general student foundation with limited funds.
