Scholar Requirements Checklist

A guide to the documents and actions required from scholars at each stage.

Onboarding (First-Time Scholars)

  • 1.

    Signed Notice of Award (NOA)

    Must be submitted with a wet (ink) signature.

  • 2.

    Scholarship Contract

    Submit 5 copies, printed on A4 paper, all with wet signatures.

  • 3.

    DBP Bank Account

    Open an account at the Development Bank of the Philippines and submit the account number to the coordinator.

  • 4.

    Withdrawal from other Scholarships

    You must withdraw from any other government scholarships, especially SLAS, as MSRS requires full tuition billing.

For Each Semester's Stipend Request

  • 1.

    Billing Statement

    Generate this from the ISKOLAR portal (or request from SRO) and have it verified by the SRO.

  • 2.

    Form 5 (Certificate of Registration)

    Your individual registration form. This must show the FULL tuition amount with NO discounts listed.

  • 3.

    TCG and COE Fees

    The university requests the True Copy of Grades and a consolidated Certificate of Enrollment for the batch. Scholars must pay the associated fee (e.g., ~‚Ç±100).

After Receiving Stipend (Liquidation)

  • 1.

    Sign the Payroll

    Crucial step. You must physically go to the designated office to sign the payroll. The process for the next semester's stipend cannot begin until this is done by everyone.

  • 2.

    Submit PhilHealth KPP Form

    Fill out and submit the Konsulta Provider Portal (KPP) form as a batch when requested. This is needed for CHED to process PhilHealth contributions.
